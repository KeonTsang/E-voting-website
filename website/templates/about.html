Understanding the First-Past-The-Post (FPTP) Voting System

----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------

The First-Past-The-Post (FPTP) voting system is a simple and widely used method for electing representatives. Here's how it works:

----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------

  1. Candidates: Multiple candidates run for a single position in an election.
  2. Single Vote: Voters cast a single vote for the candidate they prefer in their constituency.
  3. Plurality Wins: The candidate with the most votes in a constituency wins, regardless of whether they have an absolute majority.
  4. Winner Takes All: Unlike proportional representation systems, FPTP follows a "winner-takes-all" approach, where the winning candidate represents the entire constituency.
  5. Majority not Required: A candidate doesn't need to secure more than 50% of the total votes; they just need to have more votes than any other candidate.
